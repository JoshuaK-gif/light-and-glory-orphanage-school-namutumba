Our Programs

Vocational Skills Training

Equipping older children with hands-on skills for independence, productivity, and self-reliance.

Program Overview

Skills That Last a Lifetime

For older children at Light & Glory, academic education is only part of the preparation they need for adult life. Our vocational skills program introduces teenagers and young adults to practical, hands-on trades that can generate income and create a sustainable livelihood — independent of formal employment.

Tailored to the economic realities of rural Uganda, our vocational training covers areas like tailoring and garment making, basic carpentry, farming and agriculture, soap and craft making, and computer literacy. These skills are taught by experienced community practitioners in a workshop setting where learning happens by doing.

Graduates of the program leave with a portfolio of skills, a growing confidence in their abilities, and — in many cases — the tools to start a small business and begin contributing to their own future immediately.

Why It Matters

Independence Is Empowerment

  • 🔧
    A Trade Is a Safety Net

    In Uganda's economy, a practical skill can be more immediately life-changing than a certificate. A young person who can sew, farm, or build has something valuable to offer — something that feeds them and their family regardless of the job market.

  • 🚀
    Skills Create Entrepreneurs

    Many of our graduates go on to start their own small businesses. This creates a ripple effect — as they become self-sufficient and begin employing others, they carry the spirit of Light & Glory into their communities, becoming agents of transformation themselves.

  • 🏆
    Confidence Comes from Competence

    Learning to make something with your hands — to see an idea become a finished product — builds a deep sense of self-worth and capability. For young people who have known instability, that confidence is transformative and lasting.
